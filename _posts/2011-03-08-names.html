Hmm .. difficult.

The lowest quality people are who have some additional names describing their function: President, Maestro, Engineer, Father etc.

They are not individuals but Functions. Some robots with a special feature, destination.

The function is stronger than the individual itself, moreover the individual lets, by free will, even more by Desire, the function to take over (a part of) its life.

Since the function lives in many individuals it is stronger than the sum of its parts.

Then comes the real people: John, Levi and Einstein. They are nothing more than their names, and, they don’t need additional metaphors to become recognizable, part of the world.

Being simple names they have less responsibilities, lighter lives, and more chances to stay agile even when such unforeseen events occur like death, or end of the world.

And finally the lucky ones identified only by nicknames perfectly describing them: Shorty, Miss Piggy, Jack the Ripper, or Left Eye. (maybe Einstein used his nickname as public).

The nicks are perfect because they are surjective: given objectively by friends who know you better in some matters, and, happily accepted by you because you realise you are the perfect subject for that very nick.

So that’s the meaning of life: find, die, reborn and live your nick.
