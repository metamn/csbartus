Times

There are at least two types of time: the one we all know — with past and future — and another, called present, the moment when everything is happening.

Stephen Hawking showed us the linear time is finite — started about 15bn years ago and having an end somewhere. And he also says physics — thus humans in a physical universe — have less sense in a finite spacetime.

Physics managed to solve this problem by transforming the finite spacetime to something infinite like the surface of our planet by adding a new kind of time, an imaginary one.

This imaginary time has a completely different nature and direction than the real time. It is not about the far past and the far future but the endless possibilities one has in the present.

Imaginary time moves up and down instead of back and forth. It may be your mood for a given moment. Your level of spirit at a certain time.

It is strange the path physics walked through centuries: by researching the real life and existence they’ve come to add an imaginary dimension to the rescue.
